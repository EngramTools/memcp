## Decisions Made

- Export endpoint is the primary production bottleneck at scale: unbounded `SELECT * FROM memories` returns 10k+ rows with no LIMIT at concurrency 500, resulting in 30s p95. Must be paginated before launch.
- Discover endpoint excluded from performance analysis: requires embedding provider (daemon-only feature); always returns 503 in test harness. This creates a structural 40%/26%/10% error floor for 80/20/50/50/20/80 profiles respectively.
- Rate limits work correctly — high error rates in rate-limited mode at high concurrency are 429 rejections from GovernorLayer, not server failures.
- Tier mapping validated: Starter (shared-cpu-2x) for cc<=10, Growth (shared-cpu-4x) for cc<=50, Launch (performance-2x) for cc<=100, Enterprise (performance-8x) for cc<=500.

## Key Capacity Findings

**80/20 Read-Heavy Profile (primary production workload):**
- Concurrency 10, corpus 1k: 709 raw ops/sec, 19ms search p95 — Starter tier
- Concurrency 50, corpus 1k: 956 raw ops/sec, 59ms search p95 — Growth tier
- Concurrency 10, corpus 10k: 109 raw ops/sec, 99ms search p95 — search still <100ms at Starter
- Concurrency 500, corpus 10k: export p95 = 30,293ms — **breaking point** (export needs pagination)

**Cross-profile observations:**
- Search p95 scales approximately linearly: 14ms → 19ms → 99ms for 100 → 1k → 10k memories at cc=10
- Write-heavy (20/80) achieves highest raw throughput at low concurrency (1,006 ops/sec at cc=10)
- Rate limits add ~5-50ms search p95 overhead at low concurrency but stabilize behavior at high concurrency

## Issues Encountered
